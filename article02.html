KEY FACTS ON DEMENTIA

Dementia is a syndrome, usually of a chronic or progressive nature, caused by a variety of brain illnesses that affect memory, thinking, behavior and ability to perform everyday activities.

The total number of people with dementia worldwide in 2015 is estimated at 47.5 million. Among them, 58% live in low- and middle-income countries, and this proportion is projected to rise to 71% by 2050. The total number of new cases of dementia each year worldwide is nearly 7.7 million.

Did you know that:-

  • Dementia is not a normal part of ageing
  • 35.6 million people live with dementia
  • A new case of dementia is diagnosed every 4 seconds
  • Huge economic impact; US$ 604 billion per year
  • Caregivers of dementia patients experience high strain

The principal goals for dementia care are:-

  • Diagnosing cases early
  • Optimizing physical health, cognition, activity and well-being
  • Detecting and treating behavioural and psychological symptoms and
  • Providing information and long-term support to caregivers.

However, do you know that people with dementia and their families are often discriminated against?

In order to decrease discrimination and to improve the quality of life for people with dementia and their caregivers; more awareness and advocacy, research and evaluation is required. (Culled from WHO)
